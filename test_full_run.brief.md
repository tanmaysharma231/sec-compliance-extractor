--- RULE ---
Cybersecurity Risk Management, Strategy, Governance, and Incident Disclosure; Release No. 33-11216; 34-97989

--- APPLIES TO ---
The rule applies to all SEC registrants, including both domestic and foreign issuers. It specifically encompasses entities required to provide cybersecurity-related disclosures in their periodic reports, integrating oversight of cybersecurity risks by boards of directors as stipulated in Item 106(c)(1).

--- EXEMPTIONS ---
Smaller reporting companies are exempt from immediate compliance with certain aspects, as they are granted an additional 180 days from the compliance date set for non-smaller reporting companies.

--- OBLIGATIONS ---
1. [OBL-001] Registrants must disclose the occurrence of a material cybersecurity incident. Rule provision: Form 8-K Item 1.05. Deadline: 4 business days.
2. [OBL-002] Registrants may delay disclosure if the Attorney General determines it poses a substantial risk to national security or public safety. Rule provision: Form 8-K Item 1.05. Deadline: up to 60 days.
3. [OBL-003] Registrants must annually disclose their cybersecurity risk management strategies. Rule provision: 17 CFR 229.106. Deadline: annual.
4. [OBL-004] If initial Form 8-K was incomplete, file an amendment with additional information once available. Rule provision: Form 8-K Item 1.05. Deadline: 4 business days.
5. [OBL-005] Compliance with structured data requirements by tagging disclosures in Inline XBRL. Rule provision: Item 1.05 Form 8-K. Deadline: 1 year.

--- KEY DEFINITIONS ---
Cybersecurity incident means an unauthorized occurrence, or a series of related unauthorized occurrences, on or conducted through a registrant’s information systems that jeopardizes the confidentiality, integrity, or availability of a registrant’s information systems.

--- SCOPE MODIFIERS ---
Under Item 106(c)(1), registrants must describe the board's oversight of risks from cybersecurity threats, thereby expanding disclosure requirements related to governance structures and responsibilities concerning cybersecurity.

--- IMPLIED REQUIREMENTS ---
Some commenters supported requiring detailed information under Item 106(b), implying an expectation that registrants provide critical cybersecurity-related information to aid investor decision-making.

--- EFFECTIVE DATES ---
The final rules are effective September 5, 2023. With respect to Item 106 of Regulation S-K and item 16K of Form 20-F, all registrants must comply beginning with annual reports for fiscal years ending on or after December 15, 2023. Compliance with incident disclosure in Item 1.05 of Form 8-K begins on December 18, 2023, for non-smaller reporting companies; smaller reporting companies have until June 15, 2024. Inline XBRL tagging for Item 106 of Regulation S-K and item 16K of Form 20-F begins with annual reports ending on or after December 15, 2024, and for Form 8-K and Form 6-K on December 18, 2024.

--- EDGE CASES ---

--- WHAT IT MEANS IN PRACTICE ---
For OBL-001, registrants must swiftly assess and disclose material cybersecurity incidents using Form 8-K within four business days. This necessitates an internal process that involves legal, cybersecurity, and disclosure teams for evaluating and making timely disclosures.

For OBL-002, in cases where the Attorney General identifies substantial risks to national security or public safety, registrants can delay disclosure. This requires companies to have robust communications with legal and disclosure teams for appropriate coordination.

For OBL-003, annual disclosures on cybersecurity risk management strategies necessitate detailed descriptions of how companies assess and manage risks. Companies should coordinate between cybersecurity and compliance teams to maintain thorough risk management documentation.

For OBL-004, companies must track additional information availability post-Form 8-K filing. They should have processes to update filings within four business days if the initial disclosure was incomplete, bringing together expertise from cybersecurity, legal, and disclosure teams.

For OBL-005, companies must adapt their disclosure processes to incorporate Inline XBRL tagging, ensuring accessibility and transparency. This involves cooperation between legal and disclosure teams to redesign filing procedures to meet the new structured data requirements.
